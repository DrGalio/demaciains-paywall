# Gym & Fitness Studio AI Agent — Setup Guide

Get your AI fitness assistant running in under 15 minutes. Works with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

---

## What's Included in This Pack

| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| `system-prompt.md` | The AI's personality, knowledge, and behavior rules |
| `knowledge-base.md` | Customizable gym info template (fill in your details) |
| `conversation-starters.md` | 10 ready-to-use member interaction prompts |
| `setup-guide.md` | This file — step-by-step setup instructions |

---

## Option A: ChatGPT Custom GPT (Recommended)

1. Go to **chat.openai.com** → Click your profile → **My GPTs** → **Create a GPT**
2. In the **Instructions** field, paste the entire contents of `system-prompt.md`
3. Below the system prompt, add: `## Gym Knowledge Base` and paste your customized `knowledge-base.md`
4. In the **Conversation Starters** section, paste the 10 starters from `conversation-starters.md`
5. Set a name: e.g., "FitLife Gym Assistant"
6. Set a description: "Your gym's AI assistant — classes, training, nutrition, and membership help 24/7."
7. Click **Save** → **Publish** (private to your team or public)
8. Share the link with your staff and embed on your website

**Pro tip:** In GPT Builder, upload your class schedule PDF, membership brochure, or facility map as additional knowledge.

---

## Option B: Claude Project

1. Go to **claude.ai** → **Projects** → **Create Project**
2. Name it: "Gym & Fitness Assistant"
3. In **Project Instructions**, paste the system prompt + knowledge base (combined)
4. Upload any additional docs (class schedules, price lists, trainer bios) to the project
5. Start a conversation and add conversation starters as needed
6. Share the project link with your team

---

## Option C: Gemini Gems

1. Go to **gemini.google.com** → **Gems** → **Create a Gem**
2. Name: "Gym Fitness AI"
3. In **Instructions**, paste the system prompt
4. Add the knowledge base as a separate section
5. Save and share with your Google Workspace team

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific in your knowledge base.** The more real data you provide (actual class times, real pricing, specific trainer specialties), the better the AI performs. Generic templates get generic answers.

2. **Test edge cases.** Ask the AI about classes you don't offer, memberships that don't exist, injuries it shouldn't handle, and scenarios where it should escalate to a human.

3. **Update regularly.** When class schedules change seasonally, when you add new trainers, when pricing changes — update the knowledge base. Stale info erodes trust.

4. **Set boundaries.** The AI should never diagnose injuries, guarantee weight loss results, or override staff decisions. The system prompt handles this, but test it.

5. **Monitor conversations.** Review AI interactions weekly (especially in the first month) to catch and correct any issues. The Custom GPT and Claude Project both show conversation history.

6. **Combine with human touch.** Use the AI for routine inquiries — class schedules, pricing, general fitness questions. Keep complex issues (billing disputes, medical concerns, complaints) for your staff.

7. **Embed on your website.** If using ChatGPT, share the GPT link or embed it. This makes your AI assistant accessible to prospects browsing your website at midnight.

8. **Train your staff.** Let your team know the AI exists and what it handles. They should know when to direct members to the AI and when to handle things personally.

9. **Use conversation starters strategically.** Place the most valuable starters first. "New member welcome" and "membership comparison" drive the most business value.

10. **Measure results.** Track how many inquiries the AI handles, how many new members convert, and how member satisfaction changes over the first 90 days.
