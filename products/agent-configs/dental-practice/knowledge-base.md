## Services & Procedures

### Preventive Care
**Routine Cleaning (Prophylaxis)**
- Duration: 45-60 minutes
- Includes: scaling, polishing, fluoride treatment
- Recommended: Every 6 months
- Cost range: $75-$200 (varies by insurance)

**Dental Exam & X-Rays**
- Comprehensive exam + bitewing X-rays annually
- Periodic exam every 6 months
- Panoramic X-ray every 3-5 years

**Sealants**
- Protective coating on molars
- Recommended for children ages 6-12
- Quick, painless procedure

### Restorative
**Fillings**
- Composite (tooth-colored) — $150-$400 per tooth
- Duration: 30-60 minutes
- Numbing: local anesthesia
- Sensitivity: 1-2 weeks normal after procedure

**Crowns**
- Duration: 2 visits (prep + placement), 2 weeks apart
- Materials: porcelain, zirconia, porcelain-fused-to-metal
- Cost: $800-$1,500 per crown
- Temporary crown provided between visits

**Root Canal Therapy**
- Duration: 60-90 minutes per visit, 1-2 visits
- Cost: $700-$1,200
- Post-procedure: crown recommended within 30 days
- Pain management: prescription or OTC ibuprofen

**Dental Implants**
- Duration: 3-6 months total (healing phases)
- Components: titanium post + abutment + crown
- Cost: $3,000-$5,000 per implant
- Requires: adequate bone density, healthy gums

### Cosmetic
**Teeth Whitening**
- In-office: 60-90 minutes, $300-$600
- Take-home trays: custom-fitted, $200-$400
- Results: 3-8 shades lighter
- Avoid: coffee, red wine, berries for 48 hours after

**Veneers**
- Porcelain: $900-$2,500 per tooth, 2-3 visits
- Composite: $250-$600 per tooth, 1 visit
- Lifespan: 10-20 years (porcelain), 3-7 years (composite)

**Bonding**
- Quick cosmetic fix for chips, gaps, discoloration
- Duration: 30-60 minutes
- Cost: $100-$400 per tooth
- Lifespan: 3-10 years

### Orthodontics
**Invisalign / Clear Aligners**
- Duration: 6-18 months
- Cost: $3,000-$7,000
- Check-ups: every 6-8 weeks
- Wear: 20-22 hours per day

### Emergency Services
**Toothache**
- Rinse with warm salt water
- Floss to remove any trapped debris
- OTC pain relief (ibuprofen preferred)
- See dentist within 24-48 hours

**Knocked-Out Tooth**
- Handle by crown only (not root)
- Rinse gently, do not scrub
- Place in milk or saliva
- Seek dental care within 30 minutes

**Broken/Chipped Tooth**
- Save any fragments
- Rinse mouth with warm water
- Apply cold compress for swelling
- See dentist same day if possible

**Lost Filling/Crown**
- Temporary filling material available at pharmacies
- Avoid chewing on that side
- Call office for earliest appointment

## Insurance & Financial

### How Insurance Typically Works
- Preventive (cleanings, exams): 100% covered, 2x per year

- Major (crowns, root canals): 50% covered
- Annual maximum: typically $1,000-$2,000
- Waiting periods: may apply for major procedures

## Patient Experience

### What to Expect at First Visit
1. Arrive 15 minutes early for paperwork
2. Bring: photo ID, insurance card, list of medications
3. Comprehensive exam + X-rays (45-60 min)
4. Discussion of findings and treatment options
5. Cleaning if time permits and no contraindications

### Anxiety Management
- Nitrous oxide (laughing gas) available
- Oral sedation options
- Noise-canceling headphones + ceiling TVs
- Longer appointment times for anxious patients
- Tell us your concerns — we adjust to your comfort level

### Post-Procedure Care
**After Fillings:** Avoid eating until numbness wears off. Sensitivity to hot/cold is normal for 1-2 weeks.
**After Extractions:** Bite on gauze 30-45 min. No spitting, straws, or smoking for 72 hours. Soft foods for 2-3 days.
**After Whitening:** Avoid staining foods/drinks 48 hours. Use sensitivity toothpaste if needed.
**After Crown Prep:** Temporary crown — avoid sticky foods. Brush gently around it. If it comes off, call us immediately.
