# Consulting AI — Conversation Starters

These 10 prompts are ready to use as suggested conversation starters in your ChatGPT Custom GPT, Claude Project, or Gemini Gems setup. They cover the most common and high-value interactions a consulting firm AI assistant should handle.

---

## 1. Initial Inquiry

**"I'm the [CEO / COO / VP of Strategy] at [Company]. We're dealing with [brief challenge]. Can you help me understand what working with your firm would look like?"**

*Purpose: Opens a new business conversation. The AI introduces the firm's capabilities, asks clarifying questions about the challenge, and guides the prospect toward a discovery session. Sets a consultative — not salesy — tone.*

---

## 2. Service Exploration

**"I've heard about management consulting but I'm not sure which type of engagement fits my situation. Can you walk me through the options?"**

*Purpose: Educates less experienced buyers on consulting engagement types (project, retainer, embedded). Helps them self-select the right model based on their needs, timeline, and budget.*

---

## 3. Pricing Discussion

**"What does a typical consulting engagement cost? I want to understand the investment before we go further."**

*Purpose: Provides transparent pricing context without being evasive. Explains pricing tiers, what drives cost, and what's included at each level. Positions pricing in terms of value and ROI, not just hourly rates.*

---

## 4. Case Study Request

**"Can you give me an example of similar work you've done? I want to see real results, not just promises."**

*Purpose: Shares anonymized case studies relevant to the prospect's industry or challenge. Demonstrates the firm's track record with specific metrics and outcomes. Builds credibility through evidence.*

---

## 5. Project Scoping

**"Here's what we need: [detailed description of the problem/scope]. How would you approach this, and what would the timeline look like?"**

*Purpose: The AI demonstrates its ability to think like a consultant — breaking down the problem, asking clarifying questions, proposing a phased approach, and estimating timeline. Shows the prospect what working with the firm actually feels like.*

---

## 6. Team Introduction

**"Who would be working on our project? I want to make sure we get senior people, not just junior analysts."**

*Purpose: Addresses a common concern about consulting firm staffing. Explains the team structure (Partner, Principal, Senior Consultants, Analysts), emphasizes senior-heavy teams, and highlights the experience of key team members.*

---

## 7. Methodology Deep Dive

**"What's your approach to [strategy / operations / digital transformation]? I want to understand how you actually do the work."**

*Purpose: Walks the prospect through the firm's methodology — Discover, Design, Deliver, Sustain. Shows structured thinking and a repeatable process that reduces risk. Distinguishes the firm from competitors.*

---

## 8. ROI Discussion

**"How do I justify this investment to my board? What kind of return can we expect?"**

*Purpose: Helps the prospect build a business case for consulting. Provides ROI frameworks, typical return multiples (5–10x), and examples of measurable outcomes. Equips the champion to sell internally.*

---

## 9. Next Steps

**"I'm interested. What happens next? How do we move from here to an actual engagement?"**

*Purpose: Converts interest into action. Lays out the clear next steps: discovery call, assessment session, proposal, and kickoff. Removes friction and makes the path to engagement feel manageable.*

---

## 10. Partnership Inquiry

**"We're looking for a long-term strategic advisor, not just a one-off project. Is that something you offer?"**

*Purpose: Opens a retainer or embedded consulting conversation. Explains the partnership model, ongoing advisory capabilities, and how long-term relationships drive compounding value. Positions the firm as a trusted advisor, not a vendor.*
